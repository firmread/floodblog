Local EU statement on the conviction of Mr. Ampon Tangnoppakul

Posted on November 29, 2011 by firm

General Press Releases
Tuesday November 29, 2011

 

Bangkok–29 Nov–EU
The European Union Delegation issues the following statement in agreement with the EU Heads of Mission in Thailand:

The EU Delegation to Thailand is deeply concerned about the court decision to convict and imprison Mr. Ampon Tangnoppakul for twenty years for violating Article 112 of the Thai Criminal Code and the 2007 Computer Crime Act.

The EU would like to reiterate the importance it attaches to the rule of law, democracy and the respect for human rights.

The EU urges the Thai authorities to ensure that the rule of law is applied in a nondiscriminatory and proportional manner consistent with upholding basic human rights, including freedom of expression.

lese majeste in Thailand

Posted on November 29, 2011 by firm

The direction of my project has just been clarified by the most terrify news in recent years in Thailand. A few days ago, a lower class old man, Ampon Tangnoppakul, 61 (aka Ar Kong, grandpa in Chinese) has been called guilty for sending 4 SMS to an official working for Ex-Prime Minister Abhisit Vejjajiva. Those 4 SMSs have a contents that criticism on royal family which is against the article 112 law (aka lese majeste). He been sentenced by the court to be jailed 5 years for each crime, that means 20 years for 4 SMS.

The lese majeste law has been an issue to debate in Thailand for a long time. It’s obviously that this law has its flaw, as it’s been used as weapon to discredit political opponent. Which is abuse this law and been understand by intellectuals both on conservative royalist side and progressive side that this law need to change. Yet, the friction in the society still strong, the passionate feeling about monarchy has rooted to their heart content, which somehow make this blind spot keep on its existence.

Even before I came here, I was one of more than 300 writers in Thailand signed a petition urging the amendment of the Lese Majeste Law which is being used to suppress freedom of expression in the country. Which our movement has no harm or political takes on the statement at all. But we just fight for free speech which should be our rights in democracy country. So I decided to continue on this approach and my takes on the project, using flooding as scenario, but to reflect a worse problem that been hiding on this land for decades and just rise to be able to see again after the flood has gone.

Project from Arduino Playground
Submitted by: Giles Hall

“Big Dripper is a concept based on Harold Edgerton’s Piddler. Edgerton’s Piddler, also known as a “Time Fountain”, uses a stroboscopic light source to highlight individual drops of water in a constant stream of liquid. With the strobe off, the stream looks like a solid cylinder of falling water. With the strobe on, and correctly synchronized with the actuation of the pump, the individual drips of water that compose the stream are exposed. The drips appear to hang in space as if frozen in time. By modulating the phase relationship between the frequency of the pump and the frequency of the strobe, the device can generate optical illusions of motion. For example, the individual drops can appear to fall slowly, or even crawl upwards.

Big Dripper expands on this idea by adding eight individually controllable pumps. By adjusting the phase relationships between the pumps and LEDs, a variety of visual effects can be achieved.”

Thanks to Katie, who pointing out interesting relationship between flooding and Rube Goldberg Machine. The more I think about it the more I like it. I had a brief research on the idea of Rube Goldberg Machine and found that it has really interesting background. How Rube Goldberg the cartoonist inspired people with that ridiculously complex system to achieve simple task. Which in the sense, is really good metaphor to critic about Thai politic in flooding situation without being too explicit. Because in term of interface of Thai politic, It seems like the flood has triggered so many crazy things in Thailand. Rube Goldberg Machine

Posted on November 17, 2011 by firm

Reuben Garrett Lucius Goldberg (July 4, 1883 – December 7, 1970) was an American cartoonist, sculptor, author, engineer and inventor.

“A Rube Goldberg contraption – an elaborate set of arms, wheels, gears, handles, cups and rods, put in motion by balls, canary cages, pails, boots, bathtubs, paddles and live animals – takes a simple task and makes it extraordinarily complicated.”

“In 1931, the Merriam–Webster dictionary adopted the word “Rube Goldberg” as an adjective defined as accomplishing something simple through complex means.”

“Rube did not build the machines he drew, but his cartoons have become an inspiration to aspiring engineers and scientists across the world. ”

“Goldberg did not share a surname with his children because of the amount of hate mail he received during World War II from the political nature of his cartoons. He ordered his sons to change their names from Goldberg for safety reasons.”

firm / flood / fall 2011

Posted on November 17, 2011 by firm

This blog is the documentation of Firm Tharit Tothong final project for Major Studio – Interface class in MFA Design and Technology at Parsons the New School For Design in Fall 2011. Since it’s the first time Firm have left his home country for such a long time, a strange relationship between he and the emerging of social networks in everyday life gets to the point that his experience seems to be surreal, especially when the major flooding happens in Central Thailand. Everyone he known in Thailand is facing this catastrophe, but for him it feels like just another narrative happens in the computer screen.

Through this project, Firm trying to express his awkward experience from his own perspective and skill sets he learnt from this semester and synthesize it into an installation – the work that he always wanted to do.
