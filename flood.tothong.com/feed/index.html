US Raises Concerns Over ”Akong” Case 

The United States has considered Thailand’s recent court rulings as ”inconsistent with international standards of freedom of expression.”

The Thai court sentenced Ampon ”Akong” Tangnoppakul to 20 years in prison for sending four short messages on the cell phone deemed offensive to the country’s High Institution, reported The Washington Post on Tuesday.

US State Department spokeswoman for East Asia Darragh Paradiso said the US has utmost respect for the Thai monarchy but has been “troubled by recent prosecutions and court decisions that are not consistent with international standards of freedom of expression.”

The European Union also has expressed concerns over Ampon’s court sentencing which was viewed as one of Thailand’s most severe cases involving lese majeste charges.

The 61-year-old Ampon denied he had sent the text messages, saying he was not accustomed to using varied functions on his cell phone and did not know the recipient of the messages, a personal secretary of former premier Abhisit Vejjajiva.

Meanwhile, Thai-born American national Joe Gordon is scheduled shortly to be verdicted by court for translating excerpts of a banned biography of His Majesty the King of Thailand entitled ”The King Never Smiles” and posting them online.

Also known as Lerpong Wichaikammat, the 54-year-old Gordon has pleaded guilty to the lese majeste charges perpetrated several years ago while in Colorado. He could be sentenced to 15 years in jail for such offenses. He was arrested in Nakorn Ratchasima after he had returned home earlier this year and denied release on bail.

The US Department of State expressed disappointment at the Thai prosecutors’ decision to lodge  the lese majeste lawsuit against Gordon, who is concurrently a US citizen and Thai national. US embassy officials based in Bangkok had insisted in vain the Thai authorities ensure Gordon’s freedom of expression would be respected and that he would receive fair treatment.

Thai authorities have banned Thailand’s access to the US-based website of Yale University Press, which published the book in 2006.

Bangkok–29 Nov–EU
The European Union Delegation issues the following statement in agreement with the EU Heads of Mission in Thailand:

The EU Delegation to Thailand is deeply concerned about the court decision to convict and imprison Mr. Ampon Tangnoppakul for twenty years for violating Article 112 of the Thai Criminal Code and the 2007 Computer Crime Act.

The EU would like to reiterate the importance it attaches to the rule of law, democracy and the respect for human rights.

The EU urges the Thai authorities to ensure that the rule of law is applied in a nondiscriminatory and proportional manner consistent with upholding basic human rights, including freedom of expression.

]]> lese majeste in Thailand http://flood.tothong.com/2011/11/29/lese-majeste-in-thailand/ Tue, 29 Nov 2011 06:34:55 +0000 http://flood.tothong.com/?p=113 Continue reading →]]> The direction of my project has just been clarified by the most terrify news in recent years in Thailand. A few days ago, a lower class old man, Ampon Tangnoppakul, 61 (aka Ar Kong, grandpa in Chinese) has been called guilty for sending 4 SMS to an official working for Ex-Prime Minister Abhisit Vejjajiva. Those 4 SMSs have a contents that criticism on royal family which is against the article 112 law (aka lese majeste). He been sentenced by the court to be jailed 5 years for each crime, that means 20 years for 4 SMS.

The lese majeste law has been an issue to debate in Thailand for a long time. It’s obviously that this law has its flaw, as it’s been used as weapon to discredit political opponent. Which is abuse this law and been understand by intellectuals both on conservative royalist side and progressive side that this law need to change. Yet, the friction in the society still strong, the passionate feeling about monarchy has rooted to their heart content, which somehow make this blind spot keep on its existence.

Even before I came here, I was one of more than 300 writers in Thailand signed a petition urging the amendment of the Lese Majeste Law which is being used to suppress freedom of expression in the country. Which our movement has no harm or political takes on the statement at all. But we just fight for free speech which should be our rights in democracy country. So I decided to continue on this approach and my takes on the project, using flooding as scenario, but to reflect a worse problem that been hiding on this land for decades and just rise to be able to see again after the flood has gone.

Project from Arduino Playground
Submitted by: Giles Hall

“Big Dripper is a concept based on Harold Edgerton’s Piddler. Edgerton’s Piddler, also known as a “Time Fountain”, uses a stroboscopic light source to highlight individual drops of water in a constant stream of liquid. With the strobe off, the stream looks like a solid cylinder of falling water. With the strobe on, and correctly synchronized with the actuation of the pump, the individual drips of water that compose the stream are exposed. The drips appear to hang in space as if frozen in time. By modulating the phase relationship between the frequency of the pump and the frequency of the strobe, the device can generate optical illusions of motion. For example, the individual drops can appear to fall slowly, or even crawl upwards.

Big Dripper expands on this idea by adding eight individually controllable pumps. By adjusting the phase relationships between the pumps and LEDs, a variety of visual effects can be achieved.”
